How the cats got their names:

At first, we named Tigger "Brooks" because he looked like a library cat. However, he was rowdy and rambunctious, like Tigger from Winnie the Pooh, so we gave him that name. (Tigger still lives up to his name.)
---
My parents thought Morticia was a boy, so we named her Thackery Binx, the cat from Hocus Pocus. However, when we learned that Morticia is actually a girl, we changed her name to Morticia from The Addams Family.

Funny quirks of Tigger and Morticia:

Whenever I walk from my room (especially after waking up), Tigger spots me. Then he meows extremely loud and "leads" me to the living room onto the couch so I can reach and pet him. He stops every two seconds to make sure I'm following him. I believe he was a rescue cat in another life.
---
One day, when I was crying, Morticia hopped onto my bed, looked me straight in the eyes, and started licking my face like a dog. I immediately stopped crying and started laughing. (This is why I call her More Tissues.)

•⩊•

Tigger and Morticia love listening to music, especially when my mom, my little brother, or I sing. I believe that cats perceive music as purring because when people sing, their vocal cords vibrate in a way that's similar to how a cat’s vocal cords vibrate when they purr.
